  • Non-Invasive Glucometer

    • I enhanced a glucose measuring device that utilizes IR spectroscopy to non-invasively measure blood glucose levels without the need for blood draws. The device was built using IR spectroscopy to detect glucose levels, and I integrated an Arduino to process the signals and display the glucose readings. The system provides a level of accuracy up to 60%, making it a valuable tool for monitoring glucose without traditional invasive methods. The project involved working with Arduino, IR spectroscopy, and various sensors to deliver a functional and innovative solution.

  • Smart Parking

    • The Smart Parking System utilizes a NodeMCU microcontroller and ultrasonic sensors to detect available parking spaces in real-time. The ultrasonic sensors are strategically placed in parking spots to measure distance and determine whether a space is occupied or vacant. The NodeMCU processes this data and communicates it to a central system or app, providing users with real-time information about available parking spaces. This system enhances parking efficiency by guiding drivers to open spots, reducing search time and congestion. The project demonstrates my expertise in IoT, sensor integration, and real-time data processing using NodeMCU, Lcd Display and Ultrasonic sensors.
